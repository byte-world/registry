# Byte World — Community Code of Conduct

Welcome to **Byte World**, an open, collaborative, and educational virtual
networking environment. This Code of Conduct outlines the expectations,
responsibilities, and standards for all participants.

Byte World is built on respect, learning, cooperation, and responsible
experimentation. By joining, you agree to follow these rules to maintain a
safe, stable, and welcoming environment for everyone.

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to:
- Ensure Byte World remains a friendly and inclusive community.
- Protect the stability of the shared network.
- Encourage learning and healthy collaboration.
- Deter harmful or disruptive behavior.

This applies to:
- The registry and all GitHub repositories.
- BGP sessions, tunnels, and infrastructure connections.
- Byte World chat groups, forums, or communication channels.
- All participants’ interactions.

---

## 2. Expected Behavior

All participants should:

### ✔ Respect Others
- Treat everyone with courtesy and professionalism.
- Use inclusive language.
- Respect differences in experience, culture, background, and opinions.

### ✔ Maintain Network Stability
- Announce only your own prefixes.
- Maintain your IRR objects accurately.
- Keep tunnel endpoints functional and updated.
- Notify peers of major maintenance or outages.

### ✔ Follow Ethical Experimentation
- Experiment responsibly.
- Ensure activities are safe for the Byte World network.
- Test disruptive configurations only in isolated environments.

### ✔ Communicate Openly
- Report network issues promptly.
- Cooperate during incidents.
- Engage constructively during disputes.

---

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors will not be tolerated:

### ❌ Technical Misconduct
- Flooding routes, hijacking prefixes, or breaking BGP intentionally.
- Advertising public Internet prefixes.
- Injecting malformed or harmful BGP attributes.
- Network scanning outside acceptable boundaries.
- Running DoS/DDoS attacks on any Byte World participant.

### ❌ Social Misconduct
- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Hate speech or abusive language.
- Threats, intimidation, or retaliation.

### ❌ Administrative Misconduct
- Submitting fraudulent registry objects.
- Impersonating other members.
- Sharing harmful code or malicious configurations.
- Violating confidentiality agreements with peers.

---

## 4. Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness a violation, please report it through one of:
- Opening a confidential Issue on the registry repository.
- Contacting the registry maintainers directly.
- Messaging moderators in official Byte World communication channels.

All reports will be taken seriously and investigated promptly.

---

## 5. Enforcement

Depending on the severity and frequency, violations may result in:

1. A **warning**  
2. Required corrective action  
3. Temporary removal of peering sessions  
4. Suspension of registry access  
5. Permanent ban from Byte World  

Registry administrators reserve the right to take any action necessary to
protect community safety and network stability.

---

## 6. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All public and private Byte World spaces
- All technical interactions (BGP, tunnels, IRR submissions)
- Written, verbal, and digital communication
- All contributors, maintainers, admins, and peers

---

---

## 8. Commitment

By participating in Byte World, you acknowledge and agree to uphold these
standards. Together we create a safe, stable, and innovative environment to
learn, experiment, and build.

**Thank you for helping make Byte World a great place for everyone.**
